John-David Amador was Born in Sacramento, CA

He enlisted in the United States Marine Corps. He attended Marine Corps. Recruit Training at Marine Corps Recruit Depot San Diego , California. Upon completion of recruit training Pvt Amador attended Marine Corps Combat Training at Camp Pendleton, California. Pvt Amador was assigned the military occupation specialty of 0612 Field Wireman. Pvt Amador received training for his MOS at Twentynine Palms, California.

Pvt Amador was assigned orders to 2d Battalion, 2d Marine Regiment, Camp Lejeune, North Carolina to serve as a Field Wireman for Communications Platoon, Headquarters and Service Company. Pvt Amador was promoted to the rank of PFC during deployment work-up. In April 2008 PFC Amador deployed with 2BN 2MAR to COP Rawah, Iraq and served as a Field Wireman, Switchboard Operator, and Field Radio Operator. PFC Amador was promoted to LCpl during deployment. LCpl Amador was promoted to Cpl. Cpl Amador deployed with 2D BN 2D MAR to PB Masood and LSA Shamshad, Afghanistan and served as a Field Radio Operator, BN MUX Specialist and Watch Chief for LSA Shamshad.

 

Cpl Amador reenlisted with a lateral move incentive and received training for his military occupational specialty at Twentynine Palms, California. Cpl Amador was assigned the MOS 5939 Aviation Communication Systems Technician and assigned orders to Marine Air Control Squadron 2, Marine Corps Air Station Cherry Point, North Carolina. Cpl Amador was promoted to the rank of Sgt. Sgt Amador deployed as a detatchment of MACS-2 as the Tactical Air Operation Center to Camp Leatherneck, Afghanistan and served as Squadron Communications Chief and Squadron Anti Terrorism Force Protection Chief.

 

Sgt Amador reported to Drill Instructor school, Class 3-15, MCRD Parris Island, S.C.
